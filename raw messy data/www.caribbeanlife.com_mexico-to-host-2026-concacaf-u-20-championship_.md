# Mexico to host 2026 CONCACAF U-20 championship

![](https://www.caribbeanlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/carib_square_2013_300.png)By [Devika Ragoonanan](https://www.caribbeanlife.com/author/devika-ragoonanan/ "Posts by Devika Ragoonanan")Posted on [May 12, 2026](https://www.caribbeanlife.com/mexico-to-host-2026-concacaf-u-20-championship/)

![Estadio Nemesio Diez, Toluca, Mexico - May 6, 2026 General view of fireworks as players line up before the match.](https://www.caribbeanlife.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/2026-05-07T035327Z_774741552_UP1EM5704QSM9_RTRMADP_3_SOCCER-CONCACAF-TOL-LAF.jpg?quality=51&w=1200)

CONCACAF officially announced Mexico as the host Nation for the 2026 CONCACAF U-20 Championship from July 24 to August 9, with the tournament serving as qualification for the FIFA U-20 World Cup and the 2028 Summer Olympics.

“The CONCACAF U-20 Championship represents a crucial step in the development pathway for the next generation of talent across the region, and we are delighted to stage the tournament in a country as passionate about football as Mexico,” said CONCACAF president and FIFA vice-president Victor Montagliani.

“With qualification for the FIFA U-20 World Cup and the LA Summer Olympics on the line, we expect a highly competitive tournament.”

Montagliani thanked the Mexican Football Federation and Commissioner Mikel Arriola for their dedication and support while praising their strong commitment, adding, “We look forward to delivering a premier tournament experience for both participating teams and fans alike.”

Arriola welcomed the announcement saying, “It is a great honour for us to be part of the development of the next generation of football in our region. We look forward to welcoming all participating teams and their fans to our country, and we are confident it will be a successful tournament and a great experience, reflecting the standards of CONCACAF and football in our region.”

The 2026 CONCACAF U-20 Championship will feature 12 teams, including top-ranked nations such as the United States, Mexico, and Honduras, along with teams that qualified through regional playoffs like Antigua and Barbuda, Canada, Costa Rica, El Salvador, Hai-ti, and Jamaica.

The tournament will decide four CONCACAF representatives for the FIFA U-20 World Cup in Azerbaijan and Uzbekistan, while the champion will qualify for the 2028 Summer Olympics men’s football tournament. If the United States wins, the Olympic spot will go to the runner-up since they already qualify automatically as hosts.
