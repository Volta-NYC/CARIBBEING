# what our ancestors kno(e)w about our dying – an exploratory salon with Cxlture Vxlture

May 21, 7 pm – 8:30 pm. Free

![CV_Workshop-flyer-2a](https://events.amny.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/CV_Workshop-flyer-2a-e1778818889697.jpg?quality=51&w=1080)

- [Event website](https://luma.com/kse8wrwu)

## About the workshop:

​In this black salon we are looking into our ancestral knowledge and practices that honor Afro-Indigenous imagination. Whether it be our songs, teas, tinctures, stories, etc., where does our understanding of life, death, and grief carry on? How is that ancestral understanding of life and death experienced? How do we honor it?

​Guests are encouraged to bring (im)material memories, recipes, affirmations, prayers, etc. that feel most potent to them. With discussion prompts, food, drink, and music, we’ll get a conversation flowing like we’re right in our living room with our kin. Writing materials will be provided, but feel free to bring your own!

## ​About the workshop series:

​ **_what our ancestors kno(e)w about our dying_** is part of Bo’s community focused art project, **anvan ou bliye//before it’s too late…** This project seeks to (re)discover our relationships to, and with, death, transitions and grief, ultimately asking and answering, **_what does death and grief look like outside of, and in no regards to, the oppressive systems we wish to liberate ourselves from?_**

​This project is presented as part of Bo’s 2025 Lakou NOU artist residency project with [Haiti Cultural Exchange](https://haiticulturalx.org/?utm_source=luma). The [Lakou NOU](https://haiticulturalx.org/programs/artists-opportunities/lakou-nou/bo-dautruche/?utm_source=luma) Artist-in-Residence program provides artists of Haitian descent the opportunity to create and present new work and connect to historic Brooklyn neighborhoods, home to generations of Haitians and Haitian-Americans.

## ​About your host:

​Bo moves freely between writing, critique, strategy, research, and embodied practices. [Cxlture Vxlture](https://www.figma.com/deck/q2iLC19mfrjfhLECCXW8U1/cvp?node-id=1-21&t=cxPL2PDR4gILVuYy-1&utm_source=luma) is her explorative and speculative practice that focuses on communications importance in reality creation.
