# Beenie Man to headline Best of Best Music Festival

By Nelson A. KingPosted on [April 30, 2026](https://www.caribbeanlife.com/beenie-man-to-headline-best-of-best-music-festival/)

Dancehall artist Beenie Man is expected to headline the Best of the Best Music Fest, recognized as North America’s premier Caribbean music festival, on Sunday, May 24, 2026, during Memorial Day weekend at Maurice A. Ferré Park (Museum Park) in downtown Miami, organizers announced.

Beenie Man will perform along with Sizzla Kalonji, Capleton, Tarrus Riley, 450, Tifa, and Kkrytical, according to Jamaican-born entertainment publicist Ronnie Tomlinson, CEO of New York-based Destine Media.

She told Caribbean Life on Tuesday, April 28, that additional performers include Skeng, Shaneil Muir, Vanessa Bling, RajahWild, and Kraff.

Tomlinson said the 2026 staging will be presented by Grabba Leaf as the title sponsor, signaling an expanded global platform for Caribbean music and culture.

For nearly two decades, Best of the Best has served as a cultural mainstay. It brings together multiple generations through reggae and dancehall music. The move to Maurice A. Ferré Park (Museum Park) marks a new phase for the festival. It offers an expanded footprint and waterfront backdrop. The festival maintains its focus on Caribbean heritage and community.

Tomlinson said this year’s lineup features a mix of established icons and emerging acts.

She said Soca artist Voice will make his debut appearance at the festival.

“Known for a catalog of hits and a strong 2026 Carnival season, his inclusion reflects the event’s continued expansion across Caribbean genres,” Tomlinson said.

Following a 2025 collaboration, she said Grabba Leaf has expanded its role as title sponsor for the 2026 event.

“The partnership reflects the festival’s sustained audience growth and cultural relevance,” Tomlinson said.

“At Grabba-Leaf, LLC, we pride ourselves in staying true to our roots while focusing on continued growth and a commitment to excellence,” said Michael Robinson, president and CEO of Grabba-Leaf, LLC. Partnering with Best of the Best has been a tremendous opportunity to engage with a respected brand, cultural icons, and individuals who share the same values and vision for the future. We’re looking forward to this year’s celebration.”

Tomlinson said the festival is also supported by the Greater Miami Convention & Visitors Bureau, “reinforcing Miami’s position as a hub for Caribbean culture and music tourism.”
