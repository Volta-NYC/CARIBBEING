Establish your voice


Before you post, think about what voice will define
your channel. A good place to start is asking
yourself what you’re interested in, what unique
story you’re able to tell, and the viewers who will
come along for the ride.


[Learn more about finding your niche](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ob-LwY5fF7A)

Customize your channel branding


What you choose for your channel name, icon banner,
and trailer are all what we’d call branding your
channel. These decisions affect how your viewers
recognize you and get to know your channel, and
they’re all easy to customize using our easy tools.


[Watch a video about channel customization](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=s4sE7NyTvzs)

[Watch a video about choosing a channel name](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=y41nwoG8em8)

[Get Creator Tips for your channel banner and\\
profile picture](https://support.google.com/youtube?p=channel_banner_and_profile_picture_tips)

Get set up with a production plan


Before you press record, you’ll want to have a few
boxes checked, from choosing the right equipment, to
getting the lighting right and more. Choosing the
right camera, microphone, and even wardrobe can all
make sure filming is a success.


[Get Creator Tips for video equipment](https://support.google.com/youtube?p=video_equipment_tips)

[Get Creator Tips for shooting your video](https://support.google.com/youtube?p=video_filming_tips)

Make sure your videos follow our rules


Our copyright and community guidelines exist to keep

Get familiar with these policies to make sure that
what you’re posting follows our rules and reaches
your audience.

## Getting your channel off the ground

Okay, so you’ve created amazing content. Now what? Here are some
next steps for posting your videos, getting your first viewers,
and planning ahead for how to grow your audience.

Upload in YouTube Studio


Creator Studio is home base for Creators. This is
where you’ll post, add metadata, pull analytics, get
messages from YouTube, and more. It’s also where you
can track your progress toward eligibility in the
YouTube Partner Program (YPP).


Use the Studio mobile app to manage your channel on
the go. It’s useful for things like checking your
stats, responding to comments, and publishing and
managing your videos from your phone.


[Explore Creator Studio](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLpjK416fmKwQ42eDY75Q05uM0g3N9WNXU)

[Download the app for iOS](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/youtube-studio/id888530356)

[Download the app for Android](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.google.android.apps.youtube.creator)

Organize and create playlists


Once you upload, you can create playlists to guide
your viewers through your videos and get to know
your channel. Sort by format, date posted, and more.
Hint: use Studio Analytics to analyze together all
the videos in a playlist and understand how they
contribute to your channel.


[Read more about playlists](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/10232933)

Check to see how your video is doing


YouTube Analytics (YTA) shows you the data behind
your channel. Analytics can help you make sense of
trends, see what’s working (and what’s not), and
understand who’s watching your content so you can
plan how you post in the future.


[Get Creator Tips for YouTube analytics](https://support.google.com/youtube?p=youtube_analytics_tips)

[Read more about analytics](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/9002587)

[Get Creator Tips to analyze performance with the\\
content tab](https://support.google.com/youtube?p=video_content_tab_analytics_tips)

Get the latest news and features


Your YouTube Dashboard is your one stop shop for
information about new features and the latest
Creator news. Best of all, you can get info about
the opportunities to grow and earn, specifically
tailored to your channel.


[Take me to my Dashboard](https://studio.youtube.com/)
