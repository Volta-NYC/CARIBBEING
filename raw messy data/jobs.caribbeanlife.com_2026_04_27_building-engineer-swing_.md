**Position Description:**

The Building Engineer is responsible for the operation, maintenance, monitoring, and repair of

building systems including HVAC, boilers, chillers, electrical, plumbing, and life-safety

equipment. This role ensures a safe, efficient, and comfortable environment for building

occupants while complying with all applicable codes and regulations. The Building Engineer will

also be responsible for:

• Operating, monitoring, maintaining, and repairing:

- HVAC systems (chillers, boilers, cooling towers, air handlers)

- Electrical systems, generators, and lighting

- Plumbing systems and pumps

- Building automation systems (BAS/BMS)

• Performing preventive maintenance and responding to emergency repairs.

• Reading and interpreting blueprints, schematics, and technical manuals.

• Inspecting building systems to identify issues and ensure optimal performance.

• Monitoring energy usage and assisting with energy efficiency initiatives.

• Maintaining accurate logs, work orders, and maintenance records.

• Responding promptly to alarms, system failures, or occupant service requests.

• Conducting routine safety inspections and testing of equipment.

• Ensuring compliance with local, state, and federal codes (OSHA, EPA, building codes).

• Coordinating with contractors, vendors, and inspectors as necessary.

• Supporting building management during inspections and audits.

• Maintaining a clean, safe, and organized mechanical space.

**Non-Essential Functions**

• Responding to radio calls for assistance from museum staff

• Attending mandatory staff meetings

• Performing cleaning and maintenance duties when porters are not present

• Assisting with exhibit installation and deinstallation when needed

**Eligibility Requirements**

• Full-time status, 40 hours per week:

- 2:00pm-10:00pm (Friday, Saturday and Sunday)

- 10:00pm-6:00am (Monday and Tuesday)

• Ability to lift up to 50 lbs

• Ability to climb ladders, work in confined spaces and stand for extended periods

• Experience using forklifts, scissor lifts and other aerial lifts

**Position Requirements**

• 3-5 years of experience operating and maintaining commercial or industrial building

systems

• Technical or Trade School diploma or equivalent

• Strong knowledge of HVAC, mechanical, electrical and plumbing systems

• Ability to troubleshoot and repair mechanical systems

• Proficiency using computerized BAS (Building Management Systems)

• Good written and verbal communication skills

• Ability to work independently and as part of a team

Required Competencies: Must be eligible to join the International Union of Operating

Engineers Local 835. Philadelphia Grade A Engineer License required.

**Other Duties**: Assisting with outdoor maintenance as required and assisting event staff with

event setup as needed.

**Status**: Full-time

**Salary**: $39.07/hour
