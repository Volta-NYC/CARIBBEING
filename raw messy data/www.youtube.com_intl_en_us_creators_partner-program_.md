## Get support when you need it

As a creator in the YouTube Partner Program, you have access to
our dedicated Creator Support team. They are here to provide
one-to-one help across email and chat on a range of different
topics, including:


## Improving how you use YouTube

We can give you tips on technical aspects of YouTube
and using its features.


## Following policy and copyright guidelines

We can help you understand and comply with YouTube's
rules.


## Account and channel management questions

We can answer your questions about managing your
YouTube presence.


## Understanding performance

We can help you analyze your channel's performance
using YouTube's analytics tools.


## Content ID and rights management issues

We can help you resolve issues related to copyright
claims and disputes.


## Troubleshooting account issues

We can help you fix problems with your YouTube
account.


And as your channel grows, so does the level of support you can
tap into– all the way up to having your very own YouTube Partner
Manager!

## Already a Partner?

Already a YouTube Partner? Read on to discover how to make the
most of your partnership, access exclusive benefits, and
continue growing your channel.


01


Once accepted into the YouTube Partner Program, how do you
truly unlock your earning potential?


Firstly, making it to YouTube Partner is an amazing
achievement.  It’s a badge that says you’re among
the best of the best.  Many Congratulations!


To make sure Partners get the full benefit of being
in the Program, here are the following ways Creators
can get rewarded for making content such as Shorts,
live streaming and long form video.


- Advertising revenue: Earn revenue from Watch Page
ads and Shorts Feed ads.

- [Shopping](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=93z9DqfE_ek): Your fans can browse and buy products from your
store, or products you tag from other brands
through the YouTube Shopping affiliate program.

- [YouTube Premium](https://www.youtube.com/premium?ybp=Sg0IBhIJdW5saW1pdGVk4AED)
revenue: Get part of a YouTube Premium
subscriber’s subscription fee when they watch your
content.

- [Channel memberships](https://www.youtube.com/intl/en_us/join/): Your members make recurring monthly payments in
exchange for access to special perks.

- [Super Chat & Super Stickers](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o4pVILIsMHM&t=1s): Your fans pay to get their messages or animated
images highlighted in live chat streams.

- [Super Thanks](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6YpeNBACYbQ): Your fans pay to view a fun animation and get
their message highlighted in your video or Short’s
comments section.


Each feature has its own set of eligibility
requirements, so be sure to check the respective
criteria before turning on these features. If our
reviewers believe that your channel or video is not
eligible, specific features may not be available.
These extra thresholds exist for two main reasons.
The most important one is that we have to meet legal
requirements in every area where the feature is
available. Then, because we want to reward good
creators, we need to make sure we have enough
context on your channel. Generally, this context
means we need more content to review.


Keep in mind that we constantly review channels to
make sure your content is in line with our policies.


To start making the most of ad revenue, we recommend
do the following:


- Learn about
[AdSense](https://adsense.google.com/intl/en_us/start/), Google’s program that lets YouTube Partners
creators in YPP get paid.

- Choose how you want to monetize, how ads show up
on your videos, and how to set your default ad
formats.

- Review our advertiser-friendly content guidelines
to make sure all your content is appropriate for
ads.

- Check out our
[YouTube Self-Certification overview](https://support.google.com/youtube/answer/7687980)
so you can self-rate your videos according to our
advertiser-friendly content guidelines. This
rating helps YouTube make monetization decisions
faster and more accurately.


02


Once you’re set up in YPP, you can monitor your
performance and revenue


It may be helpful to check when your channel has
made the most money. You can check this data by
changing the date range to lifetime on the Revenue
tab in YouTube Analytics. Are there correlations
between the highest viewed days and most ad revenue
earned?


You can identify the geography where most of your
estimated revenue comes from by using expanded
analytics reports. You can create videos targeting
these specific geographies and add captions to help
reach your viewers.


To identify seasonal trends, compare your traffic
and earnings between different months or years. For
example, US advertisers spend slightly more during
the year-end holiday months, November and December.
When you compare different years, how do your
earnings and views change during certain months?


These tips can help you identify trends and patterns
in your revenue sources. View your final earnings by
logging into your AdSense account.
